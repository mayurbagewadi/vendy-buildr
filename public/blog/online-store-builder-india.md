## What to Look for in an Online Store Builder for India

Indian sellers have specific needs that global platforms often ignore. Before choosing, check for:

### UPI and Indian Payment Support
Your store must accept UPI — the way most Indians pay online. Also check for COD support (critical for Tier 2/3 cities), Razorpay and Paytm integration.

### WhatsApp Integration
India runs on WhatsApp. An online store builder for India should natively send order notifications to WhatsApp and allow customers to reach you there.

### Shiprocket or Shipping Integration
Booking couriers, printing labels, and tracking shipments should happen from inside your store dashboard — not by switching between multiple apps.

### Free Plan or Affordable Starting Price
You should not pay before making your first sale. Look for a genuine free plan or a very low-cost starting tier.

### No Transaction Fees
Some platforms take 1–2% of every sale. Over time, this adds up to significant money. Choose a platform with 0% transaction fees.

### Mobile-First Design
85%+ of Indian shoppers browse and buy on mobile. Your store must look and work perfectly on smartphones — automatically, not as an option.

### No Coding Required
You are a business owner. Your store builder should handle all technology so you can focus on products and customers.

## How to Start an Online Store in India — Common Questions Answered

### Do I need GST to start an online store in India?

No. If your annual turnover is below ₹40 lakh, GST registration is not required. You can sell on your own website without GST. You only need GST if you sell on marketplaces like Amazon or Flipkart, or if your turnover crosses the threshold.

### Do I need to register a company to open an online store in India?

No. You can sell as an individual (sole proprietor) using your personal PAN card and bank account. Company registration is optional and only needed when you want to separate business and personal finances formally.
