## Indian Ecommerce Payments — What Every Seller Must Know

### UPI (Unified Payments Interface)

UPI is India's real-time payment system. With over 300 million active users and 10 billion+ monthly transactions, it is the most popular payment method in India.

**Why UPI matters for your store:**
- Customers pay instantly via Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, and any bank's UPI app
- No card required — 90% of Indian smartphone users have a UPI app
- Instant payment confirmation — no waiting for cheques or bank transfers
- Particularly popular in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities where debit card penetration is lower

**How to accept UPI on your online store:**
Connect Razorpay to your DigitalDukandar store. Razorpay handles all UPI payments automatically. Your customers see a UPI payment option at checkout and pay via their preferred app.

**Cost:** Razorpay charges 2% per transaction. This is the standard rate across all Indian payment gateways — not a platform fee.

### Cash on Delivery (COD)

COD remains the dominant payment method in many parts of India. Despite the growth of digital payments, 40–65% of ecommerce orders in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities are still placed as COD.

**Why COD is non-negotiable for Indian sellers:**
- Buyers do not trust online stores they have never bought from before
- COD removes purchase hesitation — "I will pay when it arrives"
- Significantly higher conversion rates for first-time buyers
- Essential for clothing, jewellery, and other product categories where buyers want to verify before paying

**How COD works on DigitalDukandar:**
When a customer selects COD at checkout, the order appears in your dashboard with full delivery details. You process and ship the order. The courier collects payment at delivery. Funds are settled to your account after delivery confirmation.

**COD tips for Indian sellers:**
- Confirm COD orders via WhatsApp before shipping to reduce fake orders
- Offer a small discount (₹20–30) for prepaid orders to shift buyers to online payment
- Keep a return reserve — COD return rates are typically 15–25% higher than prepaid

### Ecommerce Platform with 0% Transaction Fee India

This is a critical distinction for Indian sellers: the difference between a **platform transaction fee** and a **payment gateway fee**.

**Payment gateway fee (unavoidable):**
Razorpay, PayU, and other payment processors charge 2% per online transaction. This is standard across all Indian payment gateways. Every online seller pays this regardless of which store platform they use.

**Platform transaction fee (avoidable):**
Some platforms add their own fee on top of the gateway fee. Shopify charges 2% on the basic plan (in addition to Razorpay's 2%). That is 4% total — ₹4,000 on ₹1,00,000 in sales, going to fees before you count shipping or costs.

**DigitalDukandar charges 0% platform transaction fee.** You pay Razorpay's standard 2% gateway fee (same as you would on any platform) but nothing extra to DigitalDukandar. This matters significantly at scale.

## Indian Ecommerce Shipping — Shiprocket and Beyond

### Shiprocket — India's Most Popular Shipping Aggregator

Shiprocket is used by over 2 lakh Indian ecommerce sellers. It aggregates shipping from multiple couriers — Delhivery, Blue Dart, Ekart, DTDC, XpressBees — and lets you compare rates, book pickups, print labels, and track all shipments from one dashboard.

**Key Shiprocket features:**
- Rate comparison across couriers — pick the cheapest or fastest for each order
- Automatic pickup scheduling from your location
- Bulk order processing — print labels for multiple orders at once
- Real-time tracking for you and your customers
- COD remittance management — funds collected on COD orders transferred to your account
- Returns management — handle reverse pickups from one dashboard

**Shiprocket pricing:**
Shiprocket charges per shipment. Rates depend on weight and distance zone. Typical rates:
- 500g within the same city: ₹50–80
- 500g to nearby state: ₹80–120
- 500g across India: ₹100–150

There is no monthly subscription for basic use. A prepaid plan reduces per-shipment rates.

**How to connect Shiprocket to your DigitalDukandar store:**
1. Create a Shiprocket account at shiprocket.in
2. In your DigitalDukandar settings, go to Shipping → Shiprocket
3. Enter your Shiprocket API credentials
4. Done — orders from your store sync automatically to Shiprocket

### Free Delivery vs Paid Delivery — What Works in India

**Offering free delivery:**
Indian buyers respond strongly to free shipping. Studies show 60%+ of Indian online shoppers have abandoned carts due to shipping charges. Offering free delivery above a certain order value (e.g., "Free shipping on orders above ₹499") increases average order value.

Build shipping cost into your product price if you want to offer "free" shipping profitably.

**Charging for delivery:**
Standard practice is ₹50–80 for small items. Be transparent about it — surprise shipping charges at checkout are the #1 cause of cart abandonment in India.

**COD charge:**
Many Indian sellers charge ₹30–50 extra for COD orders to cover the handling cost and higher return rate. This is accepted practice and helps offset the COD operational overhead.
