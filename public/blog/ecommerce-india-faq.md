## Legal and Registration Questions

### Q1: Can I sell online in India without GST registration?

Yes. If your annual turnover from online sales is below ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for some special category states), GST registration is not mandatory for selling on your own website.

However, if you sell on third-party marketplaces like Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or Snapdeal, GST registration is mandatory regardless of your turnover — those platforms require it for all sellers.

For your own DigitalDukandar store, you can start and sell without GST until your turnover crosses the threshold.

### Q2: Do I need to register a company to sell online in India?

No. You can sell as an individual using your personal PAN card and bank account. This is called a sole proprietorship and requires no formal registration.

You only need to register a business entity (Pvt Ltd, LLP, or OPC) when you want: a separate legal entity, the ability to take on investors, or formal separation of personal and business liability. These are advantages, not requirements for starting.

### Q3: Do I need a business bank account to sell online in India?

Not legally — you can receive payments in your personal savings account. However, a current account is recommended once you are doing regular volume because it has no transaction limits and makes accounting cleaner. A current account can be opened at any bank with your business registration documents.

### Q4: What licenses do I need to sell food online in India?

For home-cooked and packaged food:
- **FSSAI registration** is required for any food business (basic registration for turnover below ₹12 lakh/year — free to apply)
- GST applies if turnover crosses ₹40 lakh
- Local municipality license may be required in some cities

FSSAI registration is free, quick, and essential for any home food business in India.

2. Complete KYC with your PAN card and bank details
3. Get your API Key ID and Key Secret from Razorpay dashboard
4. In your DigitalDukandar store, go to Settings → Payments → Razorpay
5. Enter the keys and save

Your store now accepts all UPI apps (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM), debit/credit cards, and net banking. Customers see all options at checkout automatically.

### Q12: What are the payment gateway charges in India?

All major Indian payment gateways charge approximately the same:
- **Razorpay:** 2% per transaction (most popular)
- **PayU:** 1.99% per transaction
- **CCAvenue:** 2% per transaction
- **Instamojo:** 2–3% depending on plan

These fees are industry standard — not specific to any platform. There is no payment gateway in India that processes payments for free.

## Marketing and Growth Questions

### Q14: How do I rank my ecommerce website on Google in India?

Google ranking for an Indian ecommerce website requires:

**On-page SEO:**
- Use specific keywords in your product titles and descriptions (e.g., "Blue Cotton Kurti for Women — Size S-XL" not just "Kurti")
- Write unique descriptions for each product — do not copy supplier text
- Use your city or region in your store name and descriptions if you sell locally
- Create blog content targeting questions your customers ask

**Technical SEO:**
- Ensure your store loads fast on mobile (DigitalDukandar handles this automatically)
- Connect Google Search Console to your store
- Submit your sitemap to Google (DigitalDukandar auto-generates this)

**Content:**

**Local SEO:**
- Create a Google My Business profile linked to your store

### Q16: How do I get my first 100 customers for my online store in India?

Your first 100 customers come from existing relationships, not advertising:

- **Your personal contacts:** Everyone in your phone book is a potential customer or referral source. Send your store link to 50 contacts with a personal message.
- **WhatsApp status:** Post new products daily. Your status is seen by everyone in your contacts.
- **Housing society / local groups:** WhatsApp groups for your building, area, or community are warm audiences.
- **Instagram:** 3–4 posts per week with product photos and Reels showing your product in use.
- **Referrals:** Ask your first 10 customers to share your store link with someone who might be interested. Offer them a ₹50 coupon for referring a buyer.

You will reach your first 100 customers before you spend a rupee on advertising if you are consistent with these channels.

### Q17: How do I grow my online business in India after the first sales?

Growth after your first sales comes from:

1. **Repeat buyers:** Your existing customers are cheaper to sell to than new ones. Send them WhatsApp updates when new products arrive. Create a loyalty coupon (LOYAL10 for 10% off their next order).

2. **Instagram growth:** Post consistently. Reels showing your product being made or used outperform static photos. Collaborate with small influencers (5,000–50,000 followers) in your city for affordable promotion.

3. **Google organic traffic:** Write blog posts targeting questions your buyers ask. This brings free traffic month after month.

4. **Product expansion:** Add related products that your existing customers would buy. A jewellery seller adding hair accessories reaches the same customer without finding new ones.

5. **Paid advertising:** Once you have validated your product with organic sales, Facebook and Instagram ads can scale your reach. Start with small budgets (₹100–200/day) and scale only what works.

### Q18: How do I make money selling online in India?

Making money selling online in India requires three things:
1. **A product with a margin** — your selling price must cover product cost + shipping + payment fees + your time, with profit left over
2. **A platform that does not eat your margin** — 0% platform fees, low gateway fees
3. **Consistent marketing** — daily WhatsApp status, regular Instagram posting, and customer follow-up

The sellers who make serious money are not the ones with the best products — they are the ones who market consistently and follow up with customers.

## Shipping and Operations Questions

### Q19: How does shipping work for an online store in India?

The standard shipping setup for Indian sellers:
1. Connect Shiprocket to your DigitalDukandar store
2. When an order comes in, it automatically appears in Shiprocket
3. Schedule a pickup from your address
4. The courier (Delhivery, Blue Dart, etc.) picks up the package
5. Shiprocket tracks delivery and handles customer updates
6. For COD orders, the courier collects payment and Shiprocket remits it to you

### Q20: How long does shipping take across India?

Typical delivery times with Shiprocket:
- Same city: 1–2 days
- Nearby states: 2–3 days
- Across India (metro to metro): 3–4 days
- Remote areas: 5–7 days

For food products (perishable), use only same-day or next-day delivery within your city.

### Q21: How do I handle returns for my online store in India?

Standard return policy for Indian online stores: 7-day return window. Best practices:
- State your return policy clearly on your store
- Process return requests within 48 hours via WhatsApp
- Use Shiprocket's reverse pickup feature for hassle-free returns
- Inspect returned items before issuing refund
- COD return rates average 15–25% — factor this into your pricing
