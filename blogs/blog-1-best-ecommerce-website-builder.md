## What to Look for in an Ecommerce Website Builder

Before we compare platforms, here are the five criteria that matter most for Indian online sellers:

### 1. Payment Gateway Support (Especially UPI)
Most Indian customers pay via UPI, PhonePe, Google Pay, or cash on delivery. If your store does not support these, you will lose sales. Period. A builder that only integrates Stripe or PayPal is practically useless for selling in India.

### 2. Pricing That Makes Sense for Indian Businesses
A platform charging ₹2,500–₹5,000 per month only works if your store is already doing serious volume. For new sellers and small businesses, you need a free plan or an affordable starting tier that does not cut off essential features.

### 3. Ease of Setup (No Tech Skills Required)
You are a business owner, not a developer. The best ecommerce website builder should let you go from zero to live store in under an hour — without touching a single line of code.

### 4. WhatsApp and COD Integration
India runs on WhatsApp. Cash on delivery still accounts for a massive share of Indian ecommerce orders. Your platform needs to support both natively, not as expensive add-ons.

### 5. Shipping Integration with Indian Couriers
Shipping logistics in India means Delhivery, Blue Dart, Ekart, and DTDC — not FedEx or UPS. A good builder should connect with Shiprocket or similar aggregators so you can book pickups and track shipments without leaving your dashboard.

### Shopify
Shopify is the global leader in ecommerce and for good reason — it is powerful, reliable, and has thousands of apps. But it is built for Western markets first. UPI support requires third-party integrations, COD setup is clunky, and the monthly fees add up fast. For a seller doing ₹10 lakh+ per month, Shopify makes sense. For everyone else in India, you are paying premium prices for features you have to hack together.

### Wix
Wix is beginner-friendly and the drag-and-drop editor is genuinely good. But its ecommerce capabilities are limited compared to dedicated platforms. Payment options in India are restricted, inventory management is basic, and you will quickly hit ceiling on what it can do. Good for portfolios; not ideal as your primary sales channel.

### WooCommerce
WooCommerce is technically free — but only the plugin itself. You still need to pay for WordPress hosting, a theme, security, and multiple plugins to get UPI, COD, and shipping working. Total cost easily crosses ₹1,500–₹2,500/month once you add everything up, and you need some technical comfort to manage it. Not a beginner-friendly option.
