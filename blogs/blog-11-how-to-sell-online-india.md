## How to Sell Online in India — Step by Step

### Step 1: Choose What to Sell

Before you build a store, confirm there is demand for your product. The fastest way to do this:

- Check if similar products are selling on Meesho, Amazon, or Flipkart
- Post a photo on your Instagram or WhatsApp status and see if anyone asks to buy
- Ask 5 people whether they would pay ₹X for your product

Products that sell well online in India:
- Clothing and fashion (kurtas, sarees, western wear, ethnic jewellery)
- Handmade and artisan products (candles, soaps, home decor, pottery)
- Digital products (ebooks, courses, templates, printables)
- Food products (pickles, sweets, health foods, tiffin services)
- Electronics accessories (phone covers, chargers, earphones)
- Beauty and skincare (homemade or reseller)

### Step 2: Create Your Free Online Store

Go to [DigitalDukandar](https://digitaldukandar.in) and sign up with your Google account. No credit card required.

During setup:
- Enter your store name
- Choose your store category
- Add your WhatsApp number for order notifications

Your store URL is created instantly: yourstore.digitaldukandar.in

### Step 3: Add Your Products

For each product, add:
- **Clear photos:** Minimum 2–3 photos. White or clean background works best. Natural light for clothing and jewellery.
- **Product name:** Be specific. "Blue Printed Cotton Kurti — Size S to XL" sells better than "Kurti."
- **Price:** Include your cost + profit margin + estimated shipping.
- **Description:** What is it made of? What size does it fit? How should it be cared for? Answer the questions customers will ask.
- **Stock quantity:** Enter how many pieces you have so you do not oversell.

### Step 4: Set Up Payments

In your store settings, activate:
- **UPI / Razorpay:** Customers pay via any UPI app — Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM
- **Cash on Delivery:** Essential for Tier 2 and Tier 3 customers. Expect 40–60% of orders to choose COD.
- **Online banking / debit card:** Some customers prefer card payments

### Step 5: Set Up Shipping

Connect Shiprocket from your store dashboard. This lets you:
- Book courier pickups from your location
- Print shipping labels automatically
- Track all shipments in one place
- Access competitive rates from Delhivery, Blue Dart, Ekart, and DTDC

If you are selling locally only, you can handle delivery yourself and skip this step initially.

### Step 6: Share Your Store Link

Your store link works on any platform:
- **Instagram bio:** Change your bio link to your store URL
- **WhatsApp status:** Share new products and your store link regularly
- **Facebook page:** Add your store link to your page
- **Google My Business:** Add your store link to your business profile

## How to Sell Different Products Online in India

### How to Sell Clothes Online in India

Clothing is the most competitive but also the most searched category for online selling in India. To succeed:

- Invest in good product photography. Clothes on a model or mannequin sell 3–5x better than flat lay photos.
- Be very specific about sizes. Mention exact measurements, not just S/M/L.
- Show multiple colours and variants clearly.
- Offer COD — clothing buyers in India strongly prefer to see before they pay.
- Use Instagram Reels to show the fabric, drape, and fit. Link to your store in bio.

### How to Sell Jewellery Online in India

Jewellery photography is key. Use a clean white background with good lighting. Show pieces being worn where possible.

- Mention materials clearly: gold-plated, 925 silver, artificial, German silver, terracotta
- Mention weight and dimensions
- Include care instructions (especially for gold-plated and oxidised pieces)
- WhatsApp is very effective for jewellery — customers often want to ask questions before buying

### How to Sell Handmade Products Online in India

Handmade products have natural appeal in India — authenticity and craft are genuine selling points. Share your process. Photos and short videos of you making the product build trust and connect buyers to your story.

Popular handmade categories: candles, soaps, macrame, pottery, hand-painted items, crochet, resin art, leather goods.

Platform recommendation: DigitalDukandar for your own store + Instagram for discovery.

### How to Sell Digital Products Online in India

Digital products — ebooks, courses, templates, presets, printables — have zero shipping cost and infinite inventory. Once created, they sell while you sleep.

To sell digital products in India:
- Price in rupees (₹199–₹2,999 is the sweet spot for impulse purchases)
- Deliver via email or download link after payment
- Use DigitalDukandar or Instamojo for checkout

Popular digital products: social media templates, resume templates, recipe ebooks, business plan templates, workout guides, photography presets.

### How to Sell Courses Online in India

Online courses are one of the fastest-growing digital product categories in India. Formats that work:
- PDF workbooks (low price, high volume)
- Video courses on YouTube (free with upsell to paid product)
- WhatsApp groups with paid access
- Live workshop sessions (Zoom + payment link)

Price range in India: ₹299 for PDF guides to ₹9,999+ for comprehensive video courses.

### How to Sell From Home in India

Selling from home is the most common setup for Indian small businesses. You do not need an office, a physical shop, or staff. What you need:

1. A product (made at home, sourced locally, or dropshipped)
2. A smartphone with a decent camera for product photos
3. A free online store
4. A WhatsApp Business account
5. A payment method (UPI is instant and free)

Many successful Indian home businesses — tiffin services, tailoring, baked goods, handmade crafts — run entirely from home with no fixed overhead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I sell online in India without GST registration?**

Yes. If your annual turnover is below ₹40 lakhs (₹20 lakhs for some states), GST registration is not mandatory. For selling online, you need GST registration only if your turnover crosses this threshold or if you sell on marketplace platforms like Amazon or Flipkart, which require GST for all sellers. For your own online store, you can start without GST.

**Q: Do I need to register a company to sell online in India?**

No. You can sell online as an individual (sole proprietor) without registering any company. You simply use your personal PAN and bank account. When your business grows significantly, registering as a sole proprietorship, LLP, or Pvt Ltd has advantages — but it is not required to start.
