# Is Wix or WordPress Better for eCommerce? (The Answer Indian Sellers Actually Need)

Every day, thousands of Indian small business owners type this exact question into Google before spending money on a platform they will live with for years. The debate between Wix and WordPress for ecommerce is real, and the difference between getting it right and getting it wrong can cost you months of wasted time and thousands of rupees.

The short answer is that both platforms have significant limitations for sellers in India. The longer answer — which this article covers in full — explains exactly why, and what Indian sellers should consider instead.

---

## Wix for eCommerce: What Works and What Does Not

Wix positions itself as the beginner-friendly option. Its drag-and-drop editor genuinely is easy to use, and for sellers in the US or Europe, the out-of-the-box ecommerce features are reasonable. But for Indian sellers, the story is different.

### What Wix Gets Right

- **Simple setup**: Wix stores can be online within a few hours. No hosting to buy, no plugins to install, no developer needed.
- **Clean templates**: The template library is large, and the designs look professional on mobile and desktop.
- **Hosted solution**: Wix handles servers, SSL, and uptime so you do not have to think about infrastructure.
- **Customer support**: Wix has a help centre and live chat, which newer sellers often need.

### Where Wix Falls Short for Indian Sellers

- **Pricing starts at $17/month (around Rs. 1,400/month)** for the Business plan, which is the minimum tier that unlocks ecommerce features. There is no permanent free plan for selling.
- **No native UPI support.** UPI is the payment method used by over 300 million Indians. Wix does not support it natively. You cannot accept UPI QR payments, UPI IDs, or apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm through Wix's standard payment flow.
- **No Cash on Delivery (COD).** COD remains the preferred payment method for a large segment of Indian online shoppers, especially in tier 2 and tier 3 cities. Wix has no built-in COD option.
- **No Shiprocket or Indian shipping integration.** Wix's shipping partners are largely US and EU-focused. Integrating Shiprocket — the logistics aggregator most Indian sellers use — requires workarounds or manual order handling.
- **Transaction fees apply** on lower-tier plans, eating into already thin margins for Indian sellers operating in a price-sensitive market.
- **Limited payment gateway options for India.** Razorpay and Paytm Payment Gateway are not natively available on Wix. Sellers typically end up using PayPal or Stripe, neither of which is optimised for Indian buyers.

## WordPress (WooCommerce) for eCommerce: What Works and What Does Not

WordPress with WooCommerce is the most used ecommerce setup in the world by raw numbers. It powers a significant share of global online stores. For technically capable sellers with a budget for hosting and maintenance, it can be a powerful option. But it comes with real complexity.

### What WordPress WooCommerce Gets Right

- **Fully customisable.** WordPress gives you complete control over design, functionality, and code. Nothing is locked behind a proprietary system.
- **Massive plugin ecosystem.** There are plugins for virtually every feature: loyalty programmes, inventory management, multi-vendor marketplaces, SMS alerts, and more.
- **Indian payment gateway support.** Razorpay, Cashfree, PayU, and Paytm have WordPress plugins. UPI payments are possible with the right gateway plugin.
- **COD is supported** via WooCommerce's built-in payment methods.
- **Shiprocket has a WooCommerce plugin** that connects your store for automated shipping.
- **Ownership.** Your data, your code, your store — nothing is locked inside a proprietary platform.

### Where WordPress WooCommerce Falls Short

- **Hosting costs money and adds complexity.** You need a VPS or managed hosting plan, which in India ranges from Rs. 300 to Rs. 2,000 per month for entry-level plans. Managed WordPress hosting that can handle traffic spikes costs significantly more.
- **High technical barrier.** Installing WordPress, configuring WooCommerce, buying a theme, installing necessary plugins, setting up SSL, configuring email — this can take a week for a non-technical seller and requires ongoing maintenance.
- **Plugin costs add up quickly.** The premium plugins that make WooCommerce usable — advanced product filters, booking systems, abandoned cart recovery, a decent page builder — often cost Rs. 3,000 to Rs. 10,000 per year each.
- **Security is your responsibility.** WordPress sites are frequently targeted by hackers. Plugin updates, security patches, and backups are entirely your job (or you pay someone else to handle it).
- **No built-in WhatsApp order management.** Sellers who handle orders through WhatsApp — the majority of Indian small business sellers — need a third-party tool or custom integration.
- **Speed and performance require expertise.** Without caching plugins, a CDN, and image optimisation, WordPress stores are slow on mobile in India. Mobile conversion rates drop sharply with each second of load time.
