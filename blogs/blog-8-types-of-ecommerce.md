# How Many Types of E-Commerce Are There? (All 6 Models Explained with India Examples)

If you are thinking about starting an online business in India, you have probably asked yourself a simple but important question: where do I even begin?

Before you pick a platform, build a website, or list your first product, you need to understand one foundational thing — what type of e-commerce business you are actually starting. The answer shapes everything: who your customer is, how you get paid, what margins you can expect, and how difficult the whole thing will be.

There are 6 main types of e-commerce. Each works differently, suits different sellers, and comes with its own set of advantages. This guide breaks all six down in plain language, with real India examples, so you can confidently decide which model fits your goals — and start moving.

---

## The 6 Main Types of E-Commerce (With India Examples)

### 1. B2C — Business to Consumer

B2C is the most common and familiar type of e-commerce. A business sells products or services directly to individual customers — people like you and me shopping for things we want.

When you buy a phone case on Amazon, order food on Swiggy, or purchase clothes from a brand's website, you are participating in B2C commerce.

**India example:** A small business owner in Pune selling handmade leather bags through their own online store on DigitalDukandar. The store owner lists products, a customer browses and places an order, payment happens via UPI or Razorpay, and the product ships via Shiprocket. That entire chain is B2C in action.

B2C is the dominant model for small businesses and solo entrepreneurs in India. It is accessible, scalable, and works for physical products, digital products, and services alike.

---

### 2. B2B — Business to Business

In B2B e-commerce, one business sells to another business. The transaction sizes are typically larger, the sales cycle is longer, and the relationship involves bulk orders, credit terms, and ongoing contracts.

**India example:** A textile manufacturer in Surat listing their fabric collections on IndiaMART and receiving purchase orders from clothing brands across the country. The manufacturer is not selling one shirt to one person — they are selling 500 metres of fabric to a garment factory.

B2B is massive in India. Manufacturing, raw materials, wholesale distribution, software services (SaaS), and business equipment all fall under this type. If your target customer is another company rather than an individual, your model is B2B.

---

### 3. C2C — Consumer to Consumer

C2C is where individuals sell directly to other individuals, usually through a third-party marketplace that facilitates the transaction. Neither party is a registered business — it is peer-to-peer commerce.

**India examples:**
- Selling a used laptop on OLX
- A homemaker reselling wholesale products on Meesho to other buyers
- Someone listing handmade jewellery on a Facebook marketplace group

C2C has exploded in India thanks to social commerce and reseller platforms. Meesho in particular built an entire economy around C2C reselling — individuals buy wholesale, mark up the price, and sell through WhatsApp and Instagram to their own network.

The barrier to entry is essentially zero, which makes C2C a popular starting point. The trade-off is low margins and dependency on a third-party platform's rules and fees.

---

### 4. D2C — Direct to Consumer

D2C is a variation of B2C with one critical difference: the brand manufactures or sources its products and sells them directly through its own channel — no middlemen, no marketplaces, no distributors.

Instead of listing on Amazon and paying their commission, a D2C brand drives customers to their own website and keeps more of the margin.

**India examples:** Mamaearth, boAt, Wakefit, and Sugar Cosmetics are all D2C brands that built massive audiences online and sell through their own platforms instead of relying entirely on retail shelves.

For small business owners and makers in India, D2C is increasingly the smart choice. You control the brand experience, the customer data, and the margin. A kurta maker in Jaipur who builds their own store on DigitalDukandar, runs Instagram ads, and ships orders via Shiprocket is operating a D2C business — without needing a VC investment or a tech team.

---

### 5. B2G — Business to Government

B2G is when a business sells products or services to a government body, institution, or public sector entity. This is also called public procurement e-commerce.

**India example:** The Government e-Marketplace (GeM) — gem.gov.in — is India's official B2G platform. Registered vendors list products and services ranging from office stationery to industrial equipment, and government departments place orders directly through the portal.

GeM has been a significant opportunity for small and medium businesses (MSMEs) in India, as the government is mandated to procure a percentage of its needs from MSME-registered suppliers. If you manufacture products or provide services that government offices, schools, or hospitals might need, B2G is worth exploring.

B2G involves longer payment cycles and significant documentation, but the order volumes and contract security can be worth it for the right business.

---

### 6. C2B — Consumer to Business

C2B reverses the traditional direction. Here, an individual offers something of value — a skill, content, data, or creative work — and a business pays for it.

**India examples:**
- A freelance graphic designer on Fiverr or Upwork selling logo design services to a startup
- A food blogger with 50,000 Instagram followers charging a brand for a sponsored post
- A software developer on Toptal consulting for an international company from Chennai

The gig economy, influencer marketing, and freelance consulting all live in C2B territory. India has one of the world's largest freelancer populations, and platforms like Freelancer.com, Toptal, and Fiverr have made C2B accessible to anyone with a marketable skill.

## Which Type of E-Commerce is Most Profitable?

Profitability depends on your position in the value chain and how much you control.

**D2C and B2C through your own store are typically the most profitable for small businesses**, because you eliminate the marketplace commission (Amazon charges 5–15% depending on category) and you own the customer relationship.

B2B can generate higher order values, but requires more trust-building, longer sales cycles, and working capital to manage bulk inventory and credit terms.

C2C has the lowest barrier to entry but also the lowest margins, since you are usually competing on price with other individuals selling similar items.

The honest answer for most Indian entrepreneurs starting out: **D2C via your own store delivers the best combination of control, margin, and scalability** at low upfront cost.

---

## Which Type of E-Commerce is Best for Beginners in India?

For someone starting their first online business in India, **B2C or D2C through your own store is the clearest starting point.**

Here is why:

- You do not need to negotiate with other businesses (unlike B2B)
- You do not depend entirely on a marketplace's algorithm (unlike C2C on Amazon or Meesho)
- You keep more margin than any marketplace model
- You can start with zero inventory using print-on-demand or dropshipping
- Setting up your own store has never been easier or cheaper

If you are a student, a homemaker, a maker, or someone with a side business idea — B2C and D2C give you the fastest path from zero to first sale without technical knowledge or large capital.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many types of e-commerce are there?**
There are 6 main types of e-commerce: B2C (Business to Consumer), B2B (Business to Business), C2C (Consumer to Consumer), D2C (Direct to Consumer), B2G (Business to Government), and C2B (Consumer to Business). Each model defines who is buying, who is selling, and the nature of the transaction.

**Q: What is the difference between B2C and D2C?**
In B2C, a business sells to consumers — but this can happen through a marketplace like Amazon or Flipkart, which acts as a middleman. In D2C, the brand sells directly through its own website or store, eliminating the middleman entirely. D2C gives you more control over your brand, better margins, and direct access to customer data.

**Q: Which type of e-commerce is best in India for small businesses?**
For small businesses and first-time entrepreneurs in India, B2C or D2C through your own online store is the best starting point.
